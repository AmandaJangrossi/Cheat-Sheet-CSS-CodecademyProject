HTML Hierarchy

What is and why it's important?

What's Hierarchy?

Content hierarchy is the form of nesting elements inside HTML document. For the elements that nests other elements, we call 'parents', and the ones who are being nested, we say 'child' elements.

We can make a element hierarchy by using divs and spans, but there are better ways to do it! HTML has their own tags to make our code more semantic and readable.

Why it's important?

Besides the fact of making our code more readable, using the correct tags when constructing our website helps the contend being more easily understandable for people with disabilities who have to use screen readers, therefore, also helps with website visibility and SEO ranking.

How can I use it?

HTML Hierarchy is something to be planned in advance! Like what was said before, is more than just structure, is a way of organizing things to be clear and objective, besides the fact that will optimize SEO and help people with some kind of disability. We can use by ordering the tags in a way that makes sense, main titles (like h1) should be used once in a page! Tags like h2 and h3,h4... should always respect their porpouse, using a h3 tag above an h2 will work anyway, but it will confuse SEO and people who use screen readers.

For a better content management and improved manuntetability, we can segment our site in parts, like we do with a human body!

First comes the head (or <header> in our case), here we gonna have the navigation links (like our menu).

Then it comes the <main> part, in here we gonna have our sections, articles, ... All of our site content. Speaking of that, we can always section our content in smaller parts, the tag <section> for example, can be used inside the <main> tag to determine the topics we are going to talk about (like sections of a periodic)

Inside of that, we can have our <article> determining the content, having its own title, paragraphs, images, lists, tables,...

Last, but not least, we have our <footer>. In there we can put our contact information, some social media links, adresses,...

Observations

TLDR; Here are some observations to keep your HTML hierarchy concise and readable:

  1. Keep an eye in your tags! The order that they're being used, makes sense in real life?
  2. Plan your website layout in advance
  3. Is not just for beauty, is for accessibility and SEO reasons
